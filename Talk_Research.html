Implementation - Training

Preprocessing

  • CBEDs taken to the power of 0.1
  • each CBED is scaled by its distance to central probe position $\Delta s$:
$$\zeta_{xy} = \frac{1}{\Delta s * 50} \quad \quad \zeta_d = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2 * \Delta s^2} * 50}$$

Augmentation

  • Poisson noise applied (different dose and configuration each epoch)
  • Random sample removal (3%)

Implementation - Training

Loss function

$$ \begin{aligned} \mathcal{L} & = \\ & \mathcal{L}_2(|\psi(\vec{k})|^2)+(\mathcal{L}_2(\sin\phi(\vec{k}))+\mathcal{L}_2(\cos\phi(\vec{k})))*3 \\ + & \mathcal{L}_2(|\psi(\vec{r})|^2)+\mathcal{L}_2(\sin\phi(\vec{r}))+\mathcal{L}_2(\cos\phi(\vec{r})) \\ + & \mathcal{L}_2(\phi_{obj}(\vec{r})) + \mathcal{L}_{xc}(\phi_{obj}(\vec{r})) \end{aligned} $$ with: $$ \mathcal{L}_2(x)=\left(x_{true}-x_{predicted}\right)^2 $$ $$ \mathcal{L}_{xc}(x)=\textrm{normalized cross correlation between } x_{true} \textrm{ and } x_{predicted} $$

Summary

Strong points

  • Only 3x3 CBEDs per probe position enables live reconstruction & no memory constraint on dataset size
  • Super resolution can be achieved
  • Good noise robustness
  • Contrast formation aligns rather well with human intuition

Limitations

  • Range of microscope parameters limited -> not general to all cases
  • Scan strategy needs some consideration, Dose rate can be limiting
  • Speed could ideally still be better
  • Idea won't benefit from event driven data formats (in this implementation at least)

Summary

  • Accurate models for many distortions were developed
  • A CNN was trained to correct all distortions in a single shot STEM image
  • Extensive simulation and quatification studies were part of the development process and model evaluations
  • Structure quantifications show significant improvements for precision and accuracy of atom positions and SCS
  • We could confirm that also in tricky scenarios the model preserves the original structure
